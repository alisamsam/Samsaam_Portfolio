🦵 Biomechanical & Neuromuscular Analysis of the Ankle

Research Project - Postural Control Part 2 (M2 Advanced Mechatronics)

📚 RESEARCH OVERVIEW

Title: "Biomechanical and Neuromuscular Analysis of the Ankle in Postural Control"

Context: M2 Collective Research Project | Polytech Annecy-Chambéry, USMB | S9 2025

Team: Saher Alreqeb & Samsaam Ali Baig | Supervised by: Laurent Tabourot

🎯 THE PROBLEM

Postural control (PCM) is critical for understanding how the body maintains balance. Disruptions in this mechanism lead to falls, especially in elderly and rehabilitation patients.

Research Gap: How do we model and validate the complex interaction between ankle biomechanics, neuromuscular signals, and postural stability?

🔬 METHODOLOGY

Two Parallel Research Streams:

Stream 1: Data Acquisition

  • ✓ Kistler Force Plate (CoP)
  • ✓ EMG Sensors (muscles)
  • ✓ ArUco markers (kinematics)
  • ✓ Python + PyQt5 + OpenCV

Stream 2: Biomechanical Model

  • ✓ Spring-damper system
  • ✓ MATLAB Simulink
  • ✓ Simscape modeling
  • ✓ 18+ iterations

Key Equation: CoP - CoM = -(I/mgh) × θ̈

📊 RESULTS & VALIDATION

🔍 KEY FINDINGS

  • Frequency matching validates spring-damper parameters
  • Underdamped behavior is physiologically realistic for balance control
  • Stability condition correctly predicted ankle strategy effectiveness
  • Ankle acts as mechanical spring-damper for postural control
  • 18+ model iterations refined accuracy and validation

📝 CURRENT LIMITATIONS

  • Passive spring-damper model (no active muscle control)
  • Fixed parameters (individual adaptation not modeled)
  • Single-joint ankle model (full leg dynamics excluded)
  • No neural time delay implementation
